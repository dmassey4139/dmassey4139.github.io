Dylan's Hobbies


Track and Field is a hoppy that I enjoyed participating in since my sophomore year attending Meade County High School. While I started off as a mediocre runner, I quickly became the fastest on my team in the 400-meter dash after one year of training. From there I went on to win many meets that I participated in throughout high school and even went to state all three years that I ran. At the end of my senior year, the relay team I was on managed to place 8th in state in the 3200-meter relay. After graduating high school, I continued to run in college at Brescia University. While at Brescia I have managed to obtain many of the school records including the: 400-meter dash, 1600-meter relay, 3200-meter relay, 200-meter relay, distance medley relay, and long jump. I have also competed in the conference championship every year that I have ran and even managed to win some conference championships in a couple of the events. While my Track and Field career has come to an end as I’m finishing up college all the memories made will stay with me for the rest of my life.

Another hobby that I greatly enjoy is playing video games in most of my spare time that I have. While I play a variety of games from first-person shooters, RPG’s, MOBA’s and so on the game I enjoy the most is League of Legends (LoL). While most of the time I will get bored with a game after playing it for so long, I have been playing League for over five years now and still get the same enjoyment out of it. While I mostly play the game for fun, there is a ranking system broken down into different tiers, (bronze, silver, gold, platinum, diamond, masters and challenger), which allows for you to see where you stand against other players in the world. On the North American server, I have managed to climb into the diamond ranking which puts me in the top 2 percent of players in North America. The next season I’m hoping to climb even higher in ranking.

Another hobby I enjoy participating in is Ultimate Frisbee. The objective for each team is to score points by getting it into the other team’s end zone. There are a variety of rules and regulations to the game that each team must follow in order to make the game fair. One of the major rules is the only way for the disc to be moved down the field is by throwing it, meaning that the player in control of the Frisbee is not allowed to run with it. The game is considered over when one team hits the set score, or time limit that is agreed upon for the game. While I have never played this sport competitively I enjoy getting out and playing with friends/family.
